HAIKU

Explanation

The haiku is a form of Japanese poetry, written in three lines of which the first line has 5, the second line 7 and the third line has 5 syllables. The haiku, in its classical form, expresses a momentary experience, sometimes linked to and inspired by Zen. The haiku is a thimble full of emotion, in which there is little room for analyses and approximate descriptions.

Example

Autumn waits
The wind blows the mind
leaves falling softly
